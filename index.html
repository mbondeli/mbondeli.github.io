Welcome to my website! I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Political Science at Yale University. Previously, I studied at the Universities of Oxford and Lausanne. I will be on the academic job market in 2024-2025.

My work is motivated by three crucial questions. First, when does democracy consolidate? In my book project, I extend dissertation research on democratic erosion in the Weimar Republic to show how anti-democratic actors can leverage their influence over powerful bureaucracies and coercive institutions to tilt the democratic playing field and undermine democratic governance. The book contrasts the Weimar experience with the Swiss case of steady democratic consolidation. I argue that state-building and democratization must be thought of as interconnected processes.

Second, when does democracy enable broad representation? My dissertation project focuses on understanding how violence and threats of violence directed at political elites affect which citizens are afforded representation. Surveys and archival data are used to answer relevant causal questions.

Third, when does democracy reduce economic inequality? I argue that by incentivizing the professionalization of politics, representative democracy raises particularly difficult challenges for parties proposing tax-and-transfer redistribution.

My research is generously supported by the National Science Foundation, the American Political Science Association, the Rapoport Family Foundation, and the Yale MacMillan Center.
